Farming Outreach

Although Rwanda has experienced significant urbanization in recent years, the majority of Rwanda still rely on subsistence farming for their livelihoods. These farmers work diligently, often from dawn to dusk, cultivating small plots of land. Their primary goal is to produce enough food to meet their families' daily needs. Despite the growth of cities and the development of infrastructure, the rural population continues to depend heavily on agriculture. They face numerous challenges, including unpredictable weather, limited access to modern farming tools, and fluctuating market prices for their produce. Nonetheless, these farmers remain dedicated to their work, ensuring their families have enough to eat.
